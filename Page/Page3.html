Wangkang Festival

The name “wangkang” itself translates to “royal ship,” a fitting moniker for the centrepiece of the festival,aim at collecting and banishing evil spirits,bring health, peace, prosperity and happiness to the state and the world.

The Wangkang festival was brought to Malacca by Hokkien traders from China and first took place in 1854. It is only organized when mediums at the Yong Chuan Tian Temple in Malacca receive a command from the Ong Yah deites.

With 22 stops throughout its 9-kilometer (6-mile) journey in the old city of Malacca, the royal ship(Wangkang), procession was steeped in religious and cultural traditions. Priests performed purification rituals at each station, ordering evil spirits and other negative forces to board the boat. A variety of floats and performers accompanied it.

Baba & Nyonya Heritage Museum

Baba Nyonya Heritage Museum is a museum in Malacca City, Malaysia.Baba Nyonya is also known as Straits-born Chinese or Peranakan are of Chinese noble descendants which adopted the Malays culture. The interesting unique blend of cultures is born after the intermarriages between the Chinese and the local Malay.

The museum was established in 1986 by Chan Kim Lay, the fourth generation of his family to reside in the large house built by his great-grandfather in 1896 in Jalan Tun Tan Cheng Lock, a street also known as "Millionaire's Row" for its luxurious houses. The museum is known for its elaborate woodwork, furniture, and porcelain.

In addition, visitors can also experience Nyonya costumes in the museum.

Jonker Street

The primary thoroughfare of Chinatown, Melaka, is Jonker Street, sometimes referred to as the Jonker Walk. It is well-known not only as a major street food hub but also for its stores that sell clothing, antiques, and souvenirs. The night market, which takes place here every Saturday and Sunday, is the main attraction on Jonker Street.

Not only does the lively Jonker Street provide mouthwatering cuisine, unique artwork, and great deals, but it also offers a lot of activities nearby. The plethora of museums, temples, and mosques—all of which draw inspiration from throughout the globe—provide travelers with ample incentive to visit them. Jonker Street seamlessly weaves together traditional customs and contemporary technology to portray Malaysia's vibrant, fast-paced, and multifaceted cultural life.
