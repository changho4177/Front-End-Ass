What are the best things to do in Langkawi ?

Langkawi Skybridge

A 125-meter-long curved pedestrian bridge, the Langkawi Sky Bridge provides an amazing (and occasionally terrifying) view over the lush Gunung Mat Cincang, the Telaja Tujuh waterfalls, and various islets that encircle Langkawi.

Set 700 metres above sea level, it's only accessible via the final station of the Langkawi Cable Car. This sturdy bridge can accommodate up to 250 people at a time. It's fitted with double steel railings, an enclosed wire mesh, and 2 triangular platforms where you can relax and enjoy the cool breeze.

As you walk across the Skybridge, the sensation of floating amidst the clouds adds an exhilarating touch to your journey.Don’t miss the opportunity to capture memorable photos and relish the sensation of walking above the world on this incredible engineering feat.

Black Sand Beach

Black Sand Beach (Pantai Pasir Hitam) offers memorable photo opportunities and a peaceful retreat from Langkawi's busy beaches. It's just 4 kilometres southwest of Tanjung Rhu Beach, around a 20-minute drive from the Langkawi Cable Car.

The black sand is a by-product of weathering & erosion of a nearby granite. The processes have caused the rock to disintegrate to release its individual mineral. The sand consists of about 65% black minerals & 35% quartz.

On weekends and public holidays in particular, folks love to hang out at Black Sand Beach.There are many shops that sell cheap souvenirs & clothing material. There is also a children’s playground & small stalls that offer finger food & local dishes like laksa. Overall, it is a good place to chill & relax with the family.

Some locals believe it to be the result of a centuries-old curse by a mermaid after a fisherman stole her ring. Others claim that it’s burned soil from rice paddy fields back in 1821.

Legenda Park

Located beside the Kuah Jetty, Lagenda Langkawi Park in a beautifully landscaped folklore-themed park. This open air ‘garden museum’ faces the Kuah Waterfront, where tourists can get a beautiful wide-angle view of the Kuah Harbour with the famous Eagle Square in the background. The 50-hectare compound houses a collection of colourful statues, stone carvings and cultural displays depicting the mythical tales of Langkawi.

You can explore on foot or hire a buggy to get around the premises. The open-air attraction faces the Kuah waterfront, hosting around 17 sculptures with signboards in English depicting Langkawi legends.

Many colourful figures of heroic giants, mythical birds, evil ogres, and beautiful princesses can be explored.
